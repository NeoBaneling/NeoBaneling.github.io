Creative Efforts

On the Verge of a Dream

One of the earliest drawings I can remember from my youth is a colorful Jurassic landscape featuring a Triceratops and Brachiosaurus whose skin patterns were designed with the functional intention of camouflage. Years later my artistic ability and design style have matured greatly. I am comfortable in several media, from pencil to digital work to film. My design style is analogous to Art Deco, with an emphasis on using color minimally and effectively, and a philosophy to "take something known and alter it slightly, to the point that it is on the verge of a dream". Below you should find several pieces that affirm this design style and demonstrate my ability in various media forms.

Eclipse

This digital drawing comes from a personal collection titled "Planet of Dreams", and is a stylistic inspiration for Mother, a large-scale conceptual universe I have been building. The drawing does a great job of expressing shape with minimal color and negative space. For example, the existence of the mountain and moon exist only because of their fading purple outline and the blackness of their bodies, which contrast with the full sky of stars.

The drawing also has an interesting symmetry about it. The top two thirds build a natural gradient from light to dark with the fading purple, but the final third disrupts this by introducing blue and red. This disruption does not break the balance of the image, however, because of one blue dot. The light blue star among a sky of purple stars keeps the symmetry of the piece intact and provides variability to the image, which leads the eye to move around rather than become bored.

No Trash Please

While the message of this piece is quite obvious, I would like to note the choices made in its design to guide the viewer through the image. A face and a hand were deliberately included in the collage to draw people's attention, as these are both symbols which the eye gravitates towards. Bold red was used on particular objects in the design whose purpose is twofold. Firstly, the color accentuates these objects which are particularly harmful for the environment and our ecosystems, making the viewer more aware of their presence and associating them with this agressive color. Secondly, these objects all contain lines and curves that redirect the eye to the center. By highlighting these objects, the eye falls on these lines and curves and follows them back to the silent face.

This mixed media photo collage piece was created in Photoshop. Several of the photographs in the piece were shot by myself, and other images were found on the internet with license for reuse with modification.

Midnight Tango

I have written, produced, directed, and edited several short films, and with each I dedicated my focus to a particular aspect of filmmaking. For Midnight Tango, that was lighting, color, and action-fueled storytelling.

The protagonist and antagonist of the film have a unique color, and this color is prevalent in their costuming and lighting.

Arthur Valentine, the protagonist, is surrounded in blue. His costuming features a blue blazer and blue gels were used to color the lights in his shots. This cool blue tone reflects his smooth, slick character. In contrast, Liz wears a violent red dress and is often lit in a bright red light. This color choice not only represents the previous romance between the protagonist and antagonist, but screams "danger". Its presence represents Arthur falling under Liz's enchantment, especially when it appears in the dancing scene.
