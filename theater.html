Midnight Tango

A study in Lighting, Costuming, and Action-Driven Story

I have written, produced, directed, and edited several short films, and with each I dedicated my focus to a particular aspect of filmmaking. For Midnight Tango, that was lighting, color, and action-fueled storytelling.

The protagonist and antagonist of the film have a unique color, and this color is prevalent in their costuming and lighting.

Arthur Valentine, the protagonist, is surrounded in blue. His costuming features a blue blazer and blue gels were used to color the lights in his shots. This cool blue tone reflects his smooth, slick character. In contrast, Liz wears a violent red dress and is often lit in a bright red light. This color choice not only represents the previous romance between the protagonist and antagonist, but screams "danger". Its presence represents Arthur falling under Liz's enchantment, especially when it appears in the dancing scene.
